Koshari

Koshari (or Kushari) is Egypt's beloved national dish, a hearty, flavorful, and affordable vegetarian meal made from layers of rice, macaroni, and lentils, topped with spicy tomato sauce, garlic-vinegar sauce (Daqqa), chickpeas, and crispy fried onions, creating a unique mix of textures and sweet, spicy, and tangy flavors, popular as street food and in restaurants alike

Foul w Falafel

Foul w Falafel" (Fava Beans and Falafel) is a classic, hearty Egyptian breakfast/meal featuring creamy, spiced foul medames (stewed fava beans) paired with crispy, savory falafel (fried chickpea/fava bean patties, called taamia in Egypt) served in sandwiches or with bread and dips like tahini, often enjoyed as an affordable, culturally significant street food or restaurant staple.
