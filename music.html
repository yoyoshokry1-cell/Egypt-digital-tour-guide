Umm Kulthum (c. 1904-1975)

she was an iconic Egyptian singer, songwriter, and film actress, known as the "Star of the East" (Kawkab al-Sharq), who became a cultural and national symbol, uniting the Arab world with her powerful voice and deep emotional interpretations, performing for decades and influencing art, politics, and identity across the Middle East. She rose from humble beginnings to become a beloved figure, famous for her monthly radio concerts that captivated millions and her deep connection to Egyptian heritage, performing patriotic songs and supporting her nation.
